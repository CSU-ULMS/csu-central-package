How does this work?

OneSearch provides a simple way to discover the digital and print collections of the Henry Madden Library.

Use OneSearch to:

  • Search for books, ebooks, articles, videos, maps, and more.
  • Discover and request materials from other CSU Libraries (using CSU+ Resource Sharing)
  • Find course materials placed in the library by your professor (course reserves)
  • Access your library account
  • Renew your checked out library materials
  • Save references to your favorites list

Want to learn more? View our OneSearch guide.

 What am I searching?

By default, with OneSearch you are searching:

  • all books, ebooks, videos, archival collections, maps, music and other media in our collection
  • course materials that have been placed in the library (course reserves)
  • articles from a variety of licensed and open source databases. Please note that not all our licensed databases are accessible from OneSearch (see list of databases in OneSearch)

Additionally, you can expand your search to all CSU libraries with CSU+ Resource Sharing and request materials (requests usually take 2-5 business days to arrive).
I can’t find a specific item—what next?

Books and other media

If you can’t find your item at Fresno State, expand your search to all CSU libraries with CSU+ Resource Sharing. If your item is available at another CSU library, you can request it and have it sent to Fresno State. Otherwise, you can use interlibrary loan to request items not available at Fresno State or other CSU libraries.

Articles

Not all articles that the library has access to can be found with OneSearch. If you can’t find a specific article in OneSearch, use our Citation Linker or Journal Title List to check whether the the library has access to the article. If not, you can request the article through interlibrary loan.

Need help finding an item? Ask a Librarian!
